## 2016: Topological phase transitions

> [!quote] Reason
> ... for theoretical discoveries of [topological phase transitions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Topological_phase_transitions "Topological phase transitions") and [topological phases of matter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Topological_phases_of_matter)

**Laureates:** David J. Thouless,  Duncan Haldane, John M. Kosterlitz

The topological Kosterlitz-Thouless pase transition occurs in the XY-model, a modification of the Ising model. In the Ising model we have neighboring spin interaction and the spins are parallel or antiparallel to a chosen direction:
$$
H = \sum_{ij} J_{ij} s^z_i s^z_j - H_z \sum_i s_i^z
$$
where $H_z$ is the magnetization. In the XY model, two components of the spin are free (the spin is in the xy-plane):
$$
H = -J\sum_{ij} \vec{s}_i\cdot\vec{s}_j - \vec{H}\cdot\sum_i \vec{s}_i
$$
In this case, there is no continuous phase transition, but a topological one (of infinite order). This is because of long-range interactions such as entanglement (e.g., large-scale structures like vortices form.)

**Context:** Phase transitions have an order according to the Ehrenfest classification: In an $n$-th order transition, the entropy as a function of the temperature is (n-1)-times continuously differentiable.

In Landau theory, the phases are characterized by their space group. If the phase transition is of order >1, both phases match at the transition and thus one group must be a subgroup of the other.


## 2017: Gravitational Waves


> [!quote] Reason
> ... for decisive contributions to the [LIGO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/LIGO "LIGO") detector and the observation of [gravitational waves](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gravitational_wave)

**Laureates:** Kip Thorne, Rainer Weiss, Barry Barish

- Gravitational waves are quadrupole radiation emitted from relatively moving masses (not spherically symmetric). They change the physical distances of objects when passing.
- **LIGO** is an interferometer with two arms. A passing waves changes the length of the arms, causing interference patterns.
- Note: There is **no gravitational dipole radiation** since the dipole moment is
$$
\vec{d} = M_\text{COM} \vec{X}_\text{COM}
$$
- with COM="Center of mass". Thus, in the COM frame, it vanishes. The quadrupole is the first non-vanishing moment.
- The first signal came from two merging black holes (~30 solar masses).
- **Einstein Telescope:** Triangle configuration (10km side length) or L-configuration (15km).

## 2018: 

> [!quote] Reason
> ... for groundbreaking inventions in the field of [laser physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laser_physics "Laser physics")", in particular "for their method of generating high-intensity, ultra-short optical pulses

**Laureates:** Arthur Ashkin, Gérard Mourou, Donna Strickland
- Ashkin developed optical tweezers (Pinzette)
- The others developed chirped pulse amplification: Amplification of ultrashort laser pulse to the petawatt scale ($10^{15}$).
- Before, beyond Gigawatt scale lasers would damage the gain medium (the medium which provides stimulated emission), e.g. generating plasma
- Chirping: The pulse is stretched, then amplified, and then compressed again

## 2024: Neural Networks & Machine Learning


> [!quote] Reason
> ... for foundational discoveries and inventions that enable [machine learning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Machine_learning) with [artificial neural networks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neural_network_\(machine_learning\) "Neural network (machine learning)")

**Laureates: John Hopfield, Geoffrey Hinton**

- Hopfield Network:
	- Fully connected recurrent (only one layer which feeds into itself; as opposed to feedforward networks)
	- Associative memory: Content is called by association
	- Recognizes saved patterns by going into a stable state representing this pattern
- Hinton popularized backpropagation for training multilayer neural networks


## 2025: Macroscopic tunneling & quantization in circuits


> [!quote] Reason

**Laureates: John Clarke, Michael H. Devoret, John M. Martinis**

**Discovery: Josephson effect**
	- Two superconducters placed near each other with an insulator in between
	- The combined materials still behave as a superconductor since the cooper pairs tunnel through the barrier
