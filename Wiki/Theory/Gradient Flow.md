
## SFTX

#### Why can we expand in the masses?

Why are there operators like $m\mathbb{1}$ in the basis? Consider the SFTX before we set the masses to 0.
- We have integrals $\int_pf(m,t,p)$ with mass dimension $n$.
- We can write the integral as $t^{-n/2}I(m,t)$ with a dimensionless $I(m,t)$.
- Expanding this is essentially an expansion in the mass: $I(m,t)=a(m^2t)+b(m^2t)^2+\dots$
We then take the mass derivatives and set $m=0$. The mass expansion is thus an effect of the SFTX.
