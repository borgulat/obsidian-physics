
## Deep inelastic scattering (DIS)

A lepton is scattered off a hadron:
![[Pasted image 20260306183123.png|378]]

The partons are quasi-free in the hadron due to asymptotic freedom.

The scattering process can thus be factorized into
- Scattering of the lepton off a parton carrying a fraction of the hadron momentum (described by the PDF)
- Subsequent hadronization, described by splitting functions

Factorization of the cross section:
$$
\sigma = \sum_i f_i(x,Q^2,\mu_F^2)\otimes\sigma_i(x,Q^2,\mu_F^2)
$$
where
- $\mu_F$ is the factorization scale (energies at which factorization is viable)
- $f_i$ is the parton distribution function
- $\sigma_i$ is the parton-photon cross section
- $Q^2$ is the momentum transferred via the photon

The DGLAP equation describes the $Q^2$ dependence of the PDFs:
$$
Q^2\frac{\partial f_i(x,Q^2)}{\partial Q^2} = \int_x^1 \frac{d\xi}{\xi}P_{ij}(x/\xi) f_j(\xi,Q^2)
$$
So it expresses the Q-derivative of the PDFs in terms of the other PDFs, i.e., a parton appears differently at different scales (like a quark at one scale and a gluon-quark superposition at another).

This mixing of partons with the energy scale is described by the splitting functions $P_{ij}$.
