# Magnetic dipole
## Fundamentals

In the multipole expansion, the magnetic dipole moment is given by
$$
\vec{\mu} = \frac{1}{2}\int_V\ \vec{r}\times\vec{j} \ dV
$$
and the field generated by it is
$$
\vec{B} = \frac{3(\vec{\mu}\cdot\hat{r})\hat{r}-\vec{\mu}}{r^3} \,.
$$
In an external field $B\,$, energy, force and torque are given by
$$
E=-\vec{\mu}\cdot\vec{B} \,,\qquad \vec{F} = (\vec{\mu}\cdot\nabla)\vec{B}\,,\qquad \vec{M}=\vec{\mu}\times\vec{B} \,.
$$
This implies that a magnetic dipole gets rotated into the direction of $B_\mathrm{ext}$ my the torque and assumes its minimal energy when oriented **parallel** to the magnetic field.

For charged bodies with charge $Q$ and angular momentum $\vec{J}$, the magnetic moment is given by
$$
\vec{\mu}=\gamma\vec{J}\,,\qquad \gamma=-\frac{Qg}{2m}\,,
$$
where $m$ is the mass of the body.  $\gamma$ is the gyromagnetic ratio and $g$ is the **g-factor** or **Landé factor**.

## Larmor precession

If we apply an external field $\vec{B}$ to a charged rotating body with angular momentum $\vec{J}$, there will be a **Larmor precession**: The angular momentum axis will rotate around the $B$-field axis.

We have two expressions for the torque (from the magnetic moment and from classical kinematics):
$$
\vec{M} = \vec{\mu}\times\vec{B}=\gamma\vec{J}\times\vec{B}\,,\qquad \vec{M}=\dot{\vec{J}}
$$
$$
\Rightarrow \qquad \dot{\vec{J}} = -\gamma\vec{B}\times \vec{J} = K_{-\gamma\vec{B}}\ \vec{J} \,.
$$

We thus see that the RHS is just a multiplication with a cross product matrix which again is a generator of a rotation. The frequency (Larmor frequency) is given by the Frobenius norm of $K$,
$$
f_\mathrm{Larmor} = \frac{1}{2}\mathrm{tr}(K^\mathrm{T}K) = |\gamma\vec{B}|\,.
$$
## Zeeman effect

The electron states in Hydrogen are described by $\ket{n,l,m}$, where $n=1,...$ determine the energy, $l=0,...,n$ determine the angular momentum squared and  $m=-l,...,+l$  determines the z-component of the angular momentum, leading to a magnetic moment $\vec{\mu}=m\gamma\vec{e}_z$ . This adds the energy $E_\mathrm{Zeeman}=-\vec{m}\times\vec{B}$ to the Hamiltonian and thus splits the energy levels.


# Electric dipole

## Fundamentals

Arising from a multipole expansion, the monopole is just the charge $Q$ creating a usual Coulomb potential, and the electric dipole moment is given by
$$
\vec{d} = \int_V\ \rho\vec{r} \ dV
$$
and creates an electric potential
$$
\phi = \frac{\vec{p}\cdot\vec{r}}{r^3} \,.
$$
In an external electric field $E$, energy, force and torque are given by
$$
E=Q\phi-\vec{d}\cdot\vec{E} \,,\qquad \vec{F} = Q\vec{E}+(\vec{d}\cdot\nabla)\vec{E}\,,\qquad \vec{M}=\vec{d}\times\vec{E} \,.
$$
## Larmor precession

In contrast to the magnetic case there is **no classical Larmor precession of the EDM**. This is because there is no electric analogue of $\vec{\mu}=\gamma\vec{J}$.

CP-violating quantum effects can generate an EDM which changes the Larmor frequency, though.
